Hospital Mortality Rates and Quality Ratings Analysis

Project Title: Analysis of Hospital Mortality Rates and Quality Ratings

About the Project

This project involves analyzing the California Hospital Inpatient Mortality Rates and Quality Ratings dataset. The analysis aims to investigate the relationship between hospital quality ratings, risk-adjusted mortality rates, and geographical location. By exploring these aspects, the project seeks to provide insights into hospital performance and identify potential areas for improvement.

Research Questions and Findings

1st Research Question

Question: Are hospitals with higher quality ratings more likely to have lower risk-adjusted mortality rates for specific procedures/conditions?

Analysis: Using Python, the analysis involves plotting data for hospital ratings, mortality rates, and procedures. Mean risk-adjusted mortality rates are calculated based on quality ratings.

Findings: Generally, hospitals with higher quality ratings are associated with lower risk-adjusted mortality rates. However, exceptions exist where some high-rated hospitals have higher mortality rates for certain procedures.

2nd Research Question

Question: Are there any hospitals in California that consistently have higher or lower mortality rates than the statewide average for a particular procedure/condition?

Analysis: Using R, the dataset is filtered for California hospitals. The average risk-adjusted mortality rate for each procedure/condition is compared with statewide averages.

Findings: Some hospitals consistently have higher or lower mortality rates compared to the state average. Plots show the distribution of hospitals by comparison category.

3rd Research Question

Question: Is there any relationship between the geographical location of a hospital and its risk-adjusted mortality rate for specific procedures/conditions?

Analysis: Using R, scatterplots and regression models are used to explore the relationship between hospital location (latitude and longitude) and risk-adjusted mortality rates.

Findings: A weak correlation is found between geographical location and mortality rates. Regression analysis shows a statistically significant relationship, though the R-squared value is low.

Tools and Technologies Used

  • Programming Languages: Python, R
  • Data Manipulation and Analysis: pandas, numpy
  • Data Visualization: matplotlib, seaborn, Tableau
  • Statistical Analysis: scipy, statsmodels
  • Regression Analysis: Regression models in R

Insights

The analysis reveals a correlation between hospital quality ratings and risk-adjusted mortality rates. Higher-rated hospitals generally have lower mortality rates, though there are exceptions. Geographical location shows a weak relationship with mortality rates. The findings emphasize the importance of considering additional factors beyond geographical location and quality ratings in assessing hospital performance.
